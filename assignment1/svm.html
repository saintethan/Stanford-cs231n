Inline Question 1:¶

It is possible that once in a while a dimension in the gradcheck will not match exactly. What could such a discrepancy be caused by? Is it a reason for concern? What is a simple example in one dimension where a gradient check could fail? How would change the margin affect of the frequency of this happening? Hint: the SVM loss function is not strictly speaking differentiable

Your Answer: The non-match situation between numeric and analytic gradient is simply due to the non-linear property of maximum function at 0, i.e., the function is consecutive but not differentiable at 0.

Take the example of $f(x) = max(0, x)$.

The general first order derivative expression is:

\begin{equation} \frac{df(x)}{dx} = \lim\limits_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \end{equation}

Now, based on the definition of differentiable in calculus, i.e., iff. the first order derivative values are equavalent no matter h approached 0 from either direction along the axis, we can check whether it is correct for $x = 0$.

If h approaches $x = 0$ from its negative side, let $h = -0.001$, then the first order derivative of $f(x)$ becomes:

\begin{equation} \frac{df(x)}{dx^-} = \lim\limits_{h \to 0^-} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \frac{f(0-0.001)-f(0)}{-0.001} = \frac{0-0}{-0.001} = 0 \end{equation}

On the other hand, if h approaches $x = 0$ from its positive side, let $h = 0.001$, then the first order derivative of $f(x)$ becomes:

\begin{equation} \frac{df(x)}{dx^+} = \lim\limits_{h \to 0^+} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \frac{f(0+0.001)-f(0)}{0.001} = \frac{0.001-0}{0.001} = 1 \end{equation}

Apparently, $\frac{df(x)}{dx^-}$ does not equal to $\frac{df(x)}{dx^+}$ at $x = 0$. That's why the SVM loss function is not strictly speaking differentiable.

Some of them are hardly recognized with human eyes. People cannot recognize classes like plane, bird, cat, frog, and ship. Also, not enough training data might be another potential reason. However, as for ship, it is obvious that blue is the major background color, which is normal that ships usually on the water.

On the other hand, some classes are easier to be distinguished. For instance, the visualization of car, deer, dog, horse, and truck is rather clear, which means that the computer finds a certain pattern for these classes. Take car for example. Apparently, it has a clear contour in the picture, and red is the main color. This phenomenon appears probably because that red cars is the majorty in the training data of car. Moreover, it seems that there is a horse with two heads in the visualization of horse. This might be due to the fact that there are pictures in the training data of horse with heads either to the left or to the right. This demonstrates the importance of a proper choice of training data in deep learning for satisfying results.
